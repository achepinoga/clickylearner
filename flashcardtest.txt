Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants and certain other organisms transform light energy into chemical energy. During photosynthesis, plants capture light energy from the sun using chlorophyll, a green pigment found in chloroplasts. The process converts carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. The overall equation is 6CO2 + 6H2O + light energy = C6H12O6 + 6O2. Photosynthesis occurs in two main stages: the light-dependent reactions, which take place in the thylakoid membranes, and the Calvin cycle, which occurs in the stroma of the chloroplast.
